Who am I?

I graduated high school from the University of Toronto Schools in 2011, getting my Ontario Secondary School Diploma, as well at the University of Toronto Schools Diploma, and the Lieutenant Governor's Community Volunteer Award. After high school, I attended the University of Toronto, where I am right now, studying Engineering Science and majoring in Biomedical Systems Engineering. I love learning and trying new things, and I never let a difficult problem stand in my way.

My dreams and ambitions: I want to become a surgeon when I grow up - something that I have always dreamed of. To many, my current path in Biomedical Engineering seems a bit off from where I want to be in the future, I feel that engineering is just one of the many paths which would bring me to my destination. A lot of people ask if I regret going into engineering, but I don't. It has taught me a lot of very valuable tools and tricks which I feel would be able to make me a better person in the future, regardless if I decide to continue in engineering or not.

What I Do

During high school, my main extracirriculars was on the school's annual show where I was part of the film crew. I spent my last two years of high school as the head coordinator of filming. In my last year, I also co-founded the school's forensic club where we would introduce younger students to the world of forensic science.

My current major is Biomedical Systems Engineering (BME), with an interest in applications of materials and implants in regenerative medicine.
